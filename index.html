Front-End Development

You may want to consider Front-End Development, where the user interaction with a product is paramount. If you choose Front-End, you will be working with HTML, CSS, and Javascript, as well as helpful Front-End frameworks, which are constantly changing and improving.

Back-End Development

You may want to consider Back-End Development, where the business logic systems are built. For Back-End, you may want to continue the survey to see which programming languages may be a good fit for you to start with.

Full-Stack Development

You may want to consider becoming a Full-Stack Developer, who learns both the Back-End—where the business logic systems are built—and Front-End—where the user interaction with a product is paramount. Many developers start out in one or the other and expand their knowledge in their career. Choosing your first programming language isn't as important as you might think. Once you learn programming it will be much easier to pick up additional languages, and it may even be expected of you. Research what types of things you want to do, and find out what languages they use. Perhaps try out other languages while you are learning your first, so you can be comfortable switching between them.
